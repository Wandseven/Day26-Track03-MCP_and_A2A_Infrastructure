## Comprehensive Legal Analysis: Contract Breach and Tax Avoidance

Based on the analysis from our legal specialists, here's what you need to know:

---

### **OVERVIEW**

When a company both breaks a contract and avoids taxes, it faces **two separate sets of consequences** that can occur simultaneously:

---

## **I. CONTRACT BREACH CONSEQUENCES (Civil Law)**

### **Available Remedies:**

1. **Monetary Damages**
   - Compensatory damages to cover actual losses
   - Consequential damages for foreseeable indirect losses (Hadley v. Baxendale)
   - In rare cases, punitive damages for egregious conduct

2. **Equitable Remedies**
   - Specific performance (if subject matter is unique)
   - Injunctions (to prevent further breach, especially in NDAs)

3. **Statute of Limitations**
   - Typically 4 years for contracts under UCC § 2-725.

---

## **II. TAX AVOIDANCE/EVASION CONSEQUENCES (Criminal & Civil Law)**

### **Legal Exposure:**

1. **Criminal Penalties**
   - Tax evasion (26 U.S.C. § 7201) is a felony
   - Up to $250,000 fine and 5 years in prison
   - Failure to file: up to $25,000 fine and 1 year prison

2. **Civil Penalties**
   - 75% civil fraud penalty of the underpayment (IRC § 6663)
   - Interest on back taxes (IRS can go back 6 years or unlimited for fraud)

3. **Personal Liability**
   - Company officers can be personally liable as 'responsible persons'.

---

## **III. REGULATORY & COMPLIANCE IMPACT**

1. **Securities & Reporting (SOX)**
   - False certification of financials can lead to $5M fine and 20 years prison.
   - SEC can bar individuals from serving as officers/directors.

2. **International Exposure**
   - FBAR penalties for unreported foreign accounts (up to $100K or 50% balance).
   - FATCA non-compliance consequences.
